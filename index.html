Project Overview

What we built
and why it matters

Self-administered subcutaneous injections present significant barriers for patients — inconsistent force, improper depth, and injection anxiety. Our device standardizes delivery for improved outcomes.

⚙️

Adaptive Force Profile

The device dynamically adjusts actuator speed based on real-time pressure sensor feedback, slowing delivery when resistance increases and stopping at a defined force limit.

📡

Real-Time Monitoring

Live telemetry streams step count, elapsed time, and raw force readings — enabling precise characterization of injection dynamics across device iterations.

💉

Subcutaneous Focus

Engineered specifically for subcutaneous drug delivery, accounting for the unique tissue resistance profile and depth requirements of subdermal injection sites.

🔩

Modular Hardware

Built on an Arduino platform with a servo-driven linear actuator and analog pressure sensor — keeping the system accessible, reproducible, and easy to iterate on.

Testing & Results

BLGF Data &
Key Findings

Break-loose and glide force testing across 10 iterations revealed consistent delivery profiles within our defined adaptive force zones.

AFP Zone Calibration

Force sensor values were mapped to physical Newtons across four delivery zones, allowing the firmware to modulate actuator speed proportionally to injection resistance.

Cartridge iterations showed significantly higher break-loose force compared to syringe baselines, informing future design decisions around stopper geometry and lubrication.
